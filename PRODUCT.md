# Product

## Register

product

## Users

UK local and public-policy campaigners, funders, and campaign technologists. The primary user is a campaigner reviewing a hyperlocal campaign, its evidence, strategy, and practical resources; the conference audience must also understand the capability quickly during a live demonstration.

## Product Purpose

Campaign Factory turns several hyperlocal campaign problems into concurrent, evidence-aware Campaign Factory Runs. The Factory Gallery makes genuine agent spawning, research, hand-offs, disagreement, review, human judgement, and progressive Campaign Brief assembly visible while preserving useful campaign journeys and practical resources. Success means campaigners understand both the immediate utility and the unsettling institutional capacity of an agent factory without mistaking simulation, inference, or automation for verified political reality.

## Brand Personality

Direct, intelligent, provocative. The product should feel rigorous enough to trust, ambitious enough to provoke discussion, and plain-spoken enough for non-technical campaigners.

## Anti-references

- A generic AI chatbot or prompt library.
- A permanent operations console or monitoring dashboard.
- An agent marketplace filled with interchangeable personas.
- A literal digital-workforce fantasy that anthropomorphises model calls.
- A dense architecture diagram with the campaign hidden underneath it.
- A theatrical activity feed that implies work which did not occur.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the campaign outcome; reveal orchestration as supporting evidence.
2. Make provenance, uncertainty, inference, simulation, and approval states unmistakable.
3. Show coordination through real dependencies, disagreement, and synthesis rather than inflated agent counts.
4. Preserve hyperlocal specificity; factory scale comes from disciplined machinery, not copied local variants.
5. Keep meaningful political decisions and external actions under human control.

## Release Boundary

The multi-agent rewrite is developed and rehearsed in a Factory Development Environment that is isolated from Current Production at the application, data, secrets, spend-control, worker, and replay layers. The current functional production experience stays available until an explicit Factory Promotion decision; factory development branches must not auto-migrate or write to its database.

## Visual Boundary

Campaign Factory keeps the existing light shadcn interface inspired by the Awake Next.js template: large plain headings with selective serif italics, black pill actions, thin borders, generous campaign-content spacing, and restrained pastel accents. The agent factory appears as a temporary dark translucent technical instrument above that interface while work is active. Its sci-fi character comes from real density, semantic Work Backscroll, agent identities, connectors, source activity, hand-offs, and state changes. It must not become a separate cyberpunk dashboard, terminal replica, neon visual system, or decorative glass-card grid.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA after the conference prototype. All status and provenance distinctions should work without colour alone; controls require visible keyboard focus; animated orchestration should respect reduced-motion preferences; live Factory Events need text equivalents and accessible announcements; language must remain understandable to campaigners without technical agent-system knowledge.
